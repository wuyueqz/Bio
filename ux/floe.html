Floe

Create a unique shopping experience for Floe's products; attract the traffic and scale up the business

Problem

Floe is a start-up that provides a product for preventing housing ice dam. A big milestone for the company is that it is going to launch the first series of mature products to the market after the final test in Fall, 2019. However, as Floe's business scales up, the company's original digital platform cannot play the role of branding the product and making the order.

The company's existing website cannot provide the target service

In this way, it urgently needs a new platform to provide a unique shopping experience for the target customers.

Solution

A website that can attract the traffic and enhance functionality and usability for Floe's key audiences including its preliminary and secondary users' needs, and provide a smooth shopping experience.

A quick overview of Floe's landing page

Process

When it comes to designing, I applied the Agile methodology and divided the design process into three phases corresponding to different business scopes after I clearly defined the design objectives, the users and the wireframe. For each step, I scoped business requirements and worked closely with the co-founder to identify and evaluate the design tasks.

Website visitor data provided by Wordpress

Due to the poor performance of the traffic, I dived deeper into the website itself, using the result of the previous exercise to evaluate what resources we can utilize, and what features we need to develop.

The information architecture of the existing Floe's website

By studying the information architecture of the existing website, I understood that:

  • The current information about the product is not categorized in a clear way for different users to navigate and find the desired content;
  • The information is fragmented thus can't streamline the shopping experience;
  • Some important information is missing (e.g. how does Floe compare with other products) thus can't provide a holistic experience for the customers

Market Research

Based on the study of the existing Floe's platform, I understood what we can improve through our design. However, in terms of providing the targeted information, it is necessary to understand the potential market and user deeper. I then conducted a questionnaire consisting of three questions.

Floe's User Segments

To sum, there are three user segments:

  • First, the primary customers who know the brand Floe and directly search for Floe product;
  • Second, the secondary customers who search for the solution to ice dam but dont know Floe is an alternative;
  • Third, the secondary customers who even don't know ice dam and its harm to daily life.

To briefly capture the proportion of the different users, I quickly asked 100 people about their knowledge regarding ice damage and Floe, and get a sense of the composition of this new site's potential users.

Module 2 - Payment

Sell Plan

After we launched the online form, I shifted to the next module to design the payment experience for Floe. To do this, I first talked with the co-founder to understand what the product structure is. This helps me to design the payment experience according to the business need.

From One Click to Product Complementarity

Originally, the product was designed to sell in a package, which includes a starter bundle, an extension system for different roof size, and deicer cartridges. However, through the preliminary research by Floe, users in different geolocations with various roof types require a different amount of the extension system and deicer cartridges. Meanwhile, each part of the bundle also has a different lifespan. In this way, although One Click provides a smooth shopping experience, it cannot achieve customization.

In this way, I proposed the product complementarity. The complementarity not only allows the customer to decide the quantity of each part of the product but also meet the lifecycle of each part of the product.

User Test

We then used the prototype to test the usability internally. I applied to Think-aloud Protocols to document the user's impression in every step and ensure that the prototype covers the edge cases based on the time constraint and human resources. All the 11 participants finished the task smoothly, which validated the usability of the UI.

Feedbacks from user test

We also considered useful feedback from users, e.g:

I would like to learn the specific size of the product which could help me make the decision!

To address this problem, we then refined the design by adding the dimension of the product on a tooltip for users to check as they hover on the product images.

Development

However, when we turned the design to the developer, the feedback we received mainly focuses on the feasibility:

  • Based on the existing design, it takes too much time/budget to build a transactional system internally;
  • The need for building an independent transactional system is not urgent.

In this way, we used the following matrix including time and budget to measure the alternatives and decided to use Stripe as our third party checkout platform based on the feasibility.

Redesigned purchase UI

Recently, I presented the design proposal with the co-founders and opened the conversation regarding a customized dashboard for users to check the status of their product and order the service. We are now developing the idea regarding its UX and UI concurrent with the front-end development of the website.

Lessons Learned

From this case, I summarized the methods that I used to respond to the problem that users proposed during the different phases of the process and then synthesized the process of how to design a user-friendly landing page within three steps at a high level.

Summarized the strategies applied in the design case

The method to provide a unique onboarding experience

Meanwhile, I also summarized the three ways that I used to generate the content and attract the traffic, namely: Crawling, Indexing, and Ranking, which corresponds to the three types of actions: Discovery, Relevancy, Authority. Through these methods, I align the UX design deliverables with the business goals.

I would enhance the experience through the support of website analytic tools and user's constant input after it is officially launched.
