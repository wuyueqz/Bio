HiCraft

HiCraft brings an educational and trading experience to connect craft masters, inheritors, and customers by blockchain technology, and creates learning, making, selling and buying platform both online and offline

Problem

As a country of a long history and multicultural groups, many folk arts in China are under the risk of being lost due to lack of inheritors as a result of the unprecedented rural-urban immigration.

What problem Chinese folk art is faced with?

Meanwhile, both children and adults living in cities have shown increasing interests in learning traditional handicrafts. However, the information offered by those organizations is usually organized piecemeal by independent artisans, which fail to present a big picture of Chinese folk art development or deliver a comprehensive training program.

The need for crafts learning increases steadily by year

Solution

HiCraft is a platform (web and mobile) to let millions of craft lovers (masters, apprentices, customers) around the world get connected, both online and offline, to learn, make, sell, and buy unique handicrafts. By using blockchain technology, an individual can better plan and track his/her teaching or learning process. The detailed record will be part of the unique story behind each product made through the platform.

Process

Jiping is an artisan who is passionate about Chinese folk art. In the past years, he has visited several folk art masters in remote rural areas, documented the history and crafting process of different traditional handicrafts and founded the research agency Jiyi. On the one hand, he inherited the craftsmanship from the masters and made unique goods for sale. On the other hand, he routinely updates his website to introduce different types of folk art, and hosts workshop educating children on how to make their art piece.

Jiping is organizing crafts learning workshop for passionate learners

In 2018, he reached me to explore the potential of applying emerging technology to the field of folk arts education. Learning about his concerns, I create the platform to benefit more folk art micro businessmen and protect the treasurable cultural heritage through a series of research and design. Interview

I have conducted 10 rounds of the interview with different artists, craftsmen, and material suppliers from different regions, to understand the problem and the opportunity from an insider perspective. From the conversations, I understood that all of the different roles need an integrated crafts learning platform to:

We would like to spread the culture of our village more broadly!

I want to connect closer to the studios and ateliers to sell my materials.

I would like to rent a space for art teaching from an available platform.

I then synthesized the needs related to the platform:

  • 1. Spread the crafts knowledge/methodology;
  • 2. Streamline the learning process with material supplies
  • 3. Connect with offline resources as learning space

Competitive Analysis

I then looked into the existing products that provide the crafts learning and education service. Douban Group, our main competitor, is an online social media platform allowing the young generation to exchange their creative ideas, emerging lifestyles, fashion trends and so on. Bloggers simultaneously share folk arts tutorials, from which users can learn specific making process. Meanwhile, individuals also post advertisements to organize offline studios to teach folk arts.

The competitive study of the existing products

However, by analyzing Douban Group's mode, we found that:

  • 1. The educational materials provided by independent artisans are usually fragmented, which leads to the inconsistency of the content-the apprentices lose the big picture of learning the arts systematically;
  • 2. It lacks the connection with material suppliers;
  • 3. It does not allow people to sell folk art products, which makes an unsustainable business

This information helped us to identify the function and guide the design further.

Technology Research

In this case, since we also wanted to formalize the learning process and encourage the learners, we had several rounds of technology conversation with the blockchain technologists, to figure out whether it is an appropriate technology to be introduced to this platform. I subtracted the key information from the conversations and synthesized them into the following diagram. It showed the potential relationship between different users, align with the inner relationship between different blocks.

Design with blockchain technology

We use blockchain technology to provide services for:

  • 1. Masters who specialize in certain types of folk art, especially those in high risk of cultural heritage loss;
  • 2. Apprentices who are eager to learn about a certain type of folk art but are tangled by geographical barriers or lack of resources;
  • 3. Customers who are interested in purchasing unique folk crafts for collection or gift.

The relationship between the three roles

The role of our three types of users is interchangeable. After inheriting a type of folk art through online and offline resources, an apprentice becomes a master and can pass down the skills to novices by crafting his/her learning materials or even creating a new genre. Also, a user who starts as a customer browsing for unique goods may later develop into an apprentice as he/she finds a deeper connection with a certain type of folk art.

Synthesize

In this stage, I looked deeper into the market quantitative data and performed data analysis and business study to scope the HiCraft's business plan.

Data Analysis

On the one hand, I collected the quantitative data including the potential market's geographical distribution, the user segments, and the content of the crafts and arts-related service that are most likely to be purchased.

The market data analysis of the crafts learning experience

From the back-end data provided by Jiyi-a craft study agency, we understood that the existing readers of its articles are mainly from tier 1 and 2 cities such as Beijing, Shanghai, and Hangzhou. During the interview with our client Jiping, he also mentioned his observation across the different folk art workshops that:

"Both children and adults living in cities have shown increasing interests in learning traditional handicrafts."

According to a survey conducted by The Economic Daily, as the craft innovation industry in China increased 10.3 percent to 4.6 billion US dollars in 2014 - 2015, the number of online and offline educational organizations focusing on folk art has been rocketing in urban areas.

Wireframe

After the design workshop, I grouped the design decisions and turned to a wireframe design.

The wireframe of the HiCraft

After a couple of iterations, I designed an experience and a business model that all the users can get access to a comprehensive database of Chineses folk art and get connected to friends who share the same interest. For masters, besides finding the right inheritor and enjoying the fulfillment of sharing knowledge, they can earn extra money by hosting events and sharing studio space and equipment. For apprentices, they can better plan and track their learning process and become masters themselves through our platform.

Lessons Learned

Design and business are never isolated from each other. Through this exercise, I realized that most of the time, the product design is not about simply solving a problem that someone gives to you. Instead, we need to scope the problem itself.

Today, it is necessary for a designer to think a product design problem beyond the product itself when solving the challenge. Understanding the potential market, the user segments, the business route contributes to scoping the product more accurately and eventually making it land on the earth.
